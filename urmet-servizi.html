Guiding Urmet's Evolution: From Products to Service Innovation.

Goal

Urmet SpA, a well-established Italian building technology manufacturer, is exploring a new “servitization” scenario: a transition from primarily selling products to developing a robust, service-based offering. Their ultimate goal was to identify and implement new service ideas that would secure a sustainable competitive advantage and long-term relevance in the market.

As Finsa, our mission was to act as a strategic design partner, providing an external, objective perspective to guide Urmet through this "servitization" journey. We focused on proposing a clear roadmap and actively supporting their internal teams at every stage of this transformative process.

Role & Contributions

As a Service Design Consultant with Finsa, I aquired a role in guiding Urmet through the first discovery and ideation phases for their upcoming business model transformation:
  • Managed Team Communication: managing internal communication and organization between Urmet and Finsa operative teams.
  • Conducted User & Stakeholder Interviews: actively supported and conducted comprehensive exploratory interviews.
  • Performed Market Research analyzing competitor strategies and successful service-based business models from diverse industries.
  • Led Design Workshops: designing and facilitating workshops to generate and prioritize service ideas and ensuring collective ownership and informed decision-making.
  • Documented & Presented Findings: translating findings into clear, actionable insights and presenting them to stakeholders.

Our approach

The project is following a deliberate, five-phase framework to guide Urmet's servitization transition. This structured approach ensured we systematically moved from understanding the current landscape to defining and preparing not only new ideas but laying the ground for future developments.

The five phases were: Assessment, Idea Generation, Business Modeling, Prototyping, and Rolling-out the first service offering.

Phase 1: Understanding the Landscape (Assessment)

  • Objective: To gather comprehensive insights into Urmet's internal environment and the external market.
  • What we did: We conducted in-depth interviews with 13 internal stakeholders and 6 external comercial partners. We also performed a thorough market benchmark, analyzing competitors and relevant case studies.
  • Outcome: A foundational understanding of Urmet's context, current capabilities, challenges, and initial service opportunities.

Phase 2: Finding New Ideas (Idea Generation)

  • Objective: To translate insights into viable service concepts and identify areas of focus.
  • What we did: We mapped stakeholder needs to nearly 25 distinct service concepts. Through collaborative workshops, we prioritized the 10 most promising ideas and explored technology partnerships, including a workshop session with Google Italy.
  • Outcome: A focused set of high-potential service ideas, validated through internal collaboration.

Phase 3: Defining Impact (Business and service modeling)

  • Next Steps: To narrow down the selection to 2-3 strong service concepts that hold the highest potential for Urmet.
  • Upcoming Activities (for selected ideas):
    • Technical Feasibility Analysis: Assessing if the service can be built.
    • Economic Analysis: Developing a comprehensive financial model.
    • Service Blueprint Development: Mapping the user and operational journey.
    • Development Roadmap: Outlining timelines and estimated costs.

Key Takeaways

This project has been particularly complex because it involved a huge shift in thinking for Urmet and its stakeholders, impacting many different parts of their business and people within their network. It truly highlighted the importance of clear communication and managing change effectively throughout a large-scale business transformation.
